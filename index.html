Frequently Asked Questions

What is Audio Cradle?
Audio Cradle is a lightweight macOS app that hosts Audio Unit (AU) plugins without a full DAW. Connect your audio interface, load a plugin, and play. It handles audio routing, MIDI, device management, and plugin state — everything a guitarist or musician needs to use their plugins for practice, recording, or live performance.
Can I use Audio Cradle with any AU plugin?
Audio Cradle hosts any Audio Unit (AU) plugin, though it's optimized and most-tested with Line 6 Helix Native. Other AU effect plugins — amp sims, reverbs, delays — should work. The plugin must be installed in the standard macOS location (/Library/Audio/Plug-Ins/Components/).
What buffer size should I use?
Lower buffer sizes reduce latency but require more CPU. For live performance with guitar effects: 64 samples is a good balance for most interfaces, 128 samples is a safe choice if you hear glitches at 64, and 32 samples gives ultra-low latency but only works reliably with high-quality interfaces.
Does it work with MIDI foot controllers?
Yes. Audio Cradle routes MIDI from any connected controller to your plugin. This includes Program Change messages for switching presets, CC messages for parameter control, and bank select (CC32) for navigating setlists — perfect for hands-free live performance.
Why not just use a DAW?
DAWs are built for recording and production. If you just want to plug in and play through your plugins — for practice, jamming, or live performance — a DAW is overkill. Audio Cradle launches in seconds, restores your last state, and stays out of the way.
What macOS versions are supported?
Audio Cradle requires macOS 14 (Sonoma) or later on Apple Silicon Macs.
Does Audio Cradle work with USB hubs?
Yes, but with caveats. USB hubs can add 1–10ms of additional latency depending on the hub quality. For the lowest latency, connect your audio interface directly to your Mac. If you must use a hub, a powered USB 3.0+ hub is recommended.
